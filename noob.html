Setting the stage

I've been programming for a while now, but haven't had much interest in web development so far. Maybe dabbled a bit, but never got around to playing with it for longer than a few weeks maybe.
Anyway, I wanna approach this with a beginner's mindset.

Watching from the sidelines as a programmer who's been mostly doing C# and C++ over the years, the web dev world looks like there's lots of churn. Frameworks, tools and other stuff; it looks quite hard to stay current with what's happening.
So I consciously decide to stay away from that for the time being.

I'll be doing very basic stuff. Just HTML and CSS with very minimal scripting for now.
I think it'll be very readable in "view source" mode.

It won't be pretty at the start; probably quite the opposite. But I think being willing to suck, not overwhelming myself with choices and choosing a subtopic I'm at least slightly interested in makes learning easier.
I want to find something that is fun when learning. There's enough chores in life and I've head enough of "learning" just for "an exam" type of bullshit.

If it's not fun, I don't intend on taking it much further. I've forced myself to do stuff for far too long and it never brought me things that really matter: joy and peace of mind.
That's what I'll be looking for in this journey.

One thing I haven't decided on yet; whether I'll be updating old pages when learning something new.
Like applying a nice font for example, which I wanna do in the future.

If I didn't, progress would be much more visible.

If I did, everything would look nicer and more consistent, so reading old posts when the main page changed would be less jarring.

Anyway, I guess I'll decide that at a later point.
See you next time.
